# Staff Introduction — Start Here

> ⚠️ **The server is currently in testing.** Things may break, change, or be wiped while we
> build it out. Expect rough edges, report bugs to the dev team, and don't promise players
> that progress/data is permanent yet.

Welcome to the team. This is the shared baseline every staff member should know,
regardless of rank. Your specific role guide (Mod / Admin / Builder / Developer /
Content Creator) builds on top of this.

## Golden rules
1. **Stay in staff mode for moderation.** Use `/staffmode` (`/sm`) — it equips your tools and protects your survival inventory.
2. **Communicate.** Use `/staffchat` (`/sc`) or just toggle it on. Claim reports before acting so two staff don't double up.
3. **Log everything important.** Punishments and notes are searchable — use `/note <player> <text>` for context that isn't a punishment.
4. **Be invisible when watching.** `/vanish` (`/v`) hides you from players; great for catching rule-breakers in the act.

## Everyday commands
| Command | What it does |
|---|---|
| `/staffpanel` | Your one-stop staff GUI (reports, punish, freeze, profiles…). |
| `/staffmode` | Toggle staff tools + inventory protection. |
| `/vanish` | Become invisible to players. |
| `/staffchat <msg>` | Private staff-only chat. |
| `/stafflist` | See which staff are online. |
| `/freeze <player>` | Stop a player moving while you talk to them. |
| `/report` queue | Handle player reports (also in the panel). |
| `/helpop` queue | Answer player help requests. |

## The panels
Every role has a GUI that bundles its tools. Open yours:
- Moderators → `/modpanel`
- Admins → `/adminpanel`
- Builders → `/builderpanel`
- Developers → `/developerpanel`
- Content Creators → `/creatorpanel`
- General staff → `/staffpanel`

## Don't
- Don't use creative/op powers on the live map unless your role allows it.
- Don't punish without a reason — reasons are shown to players and logged.
- Don't share screenshots of player IPs or alts outside the staff team.

See your role guide for the full toolset.
